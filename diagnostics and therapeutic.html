Cancer Center

The Cancer Center of Cardinal Santos Medical Center is dedicated to giving the best care to cancer patients.

The Center is equipped with state-of-the-art services, such as External Beam Radiation Therapy, Intensity-modulated radiation Therapy (IMRT), 3D Conformal Radiotherapy and High-dose-rate (HDR) Brachytherapy for radiotherapy.

The Radiation Oncology Department is equipped with state-of-the-art linear accelerator and branchytherapy machines. Endoscopy Unit

The Endoscopy Unit of Cardinal Santos Medical Center is dedicated to the assessment and treatment of the interior surfaces of the gastrointestinal tract through endoscopy. Minimal invasive techniques through allows for shorter hospital stay and the absence of surgical scars.

Gastroscopy
This procedure is also known as esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EGD) , upper GI endoscopy, or panendoscopy. An endoscope is used to visualize the patient’s esophagus, stomach, and duodenum. It provides direct visualization to confirm the presence of conditions such as inflammation, ulcers, and tumors. EGD also enables the physician to perform endoscopic treatments such as biopsies, removal of polyps, and control of bleeding.

Colonoscopy
A colonoscopy is the endoscopic examination of the large bowel and the distal part of the small bowel. The procedure helps find ulcers, polyps, tumors, and areas of inflammation or bleeding. It is recommended for anyone 50 years of age and older or for anyone with a family history of colorectal cancer or polyps.

Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography (ERCP)
ERCP is a procedure that combines endoscopy and x-ray to visualize the gall bladder, bile ducts, and ducts of the pancreas under fluoroscopic guidance. It is useful in diagnosing and treating problems like jaundice or abdominal pain related to gall bladder, biliary tract, and pancreatic diseases. Therapies such as stone removal and stent insertion for biliary and pancreatic duct obstructions may be performed.

Nuclear Medicine
The Section of Nuclear Medicine is under the Department of Radiology of CSMC. Nuclear medicine is a branch of medicine that uses minute amounts of radioactive materials (called tracers) to diagnose and/or treat diseases. These include diseases of the heart, lungs, bone, kidney, endocrine system (e.g. thyroid), digestive system, as well as a number of cancers.

The section has two gamma cameras, which detect radiation from the tracers and converts these signals into images. Unlike, x-rays, CT scans and MRIs that show the structure of organs, the images from nuclear medicine techniques can show the function of the diseased organ.

To ensure the accuracy and safety of all procedures, the staff and specialists of the section are licensed and certified by the Philippine Nuclear Research Institute and the Philippine Society of Nuclear Medicine (PSNM).

Research Center

Vision
The Cardinal Santos Medical Center Research Center’s vision is to create a Medical Research Environment of Excellence and Ethics (MREEE) among all its medical staff and allied medical employees, to reach out and to influence the wider national and global research community.

Mission
The Cardinal Santos Medical Center Research Center’s vision is to create a Medical Research Environment of Excellence and Ethics (MREEE) among all its medical staff and allied medical employees, to reach out and to influence the wider national and global research community.

The primary functions of the CSMC Research Center (CSMC-RC) are the following:

  • To be the receiving office for all trainee or medical personnel–initiated applications for permission to conduct medical researches within CSMC
  • To archive an electronic copy of all approved original medical trainee or medical consultant-initiated academic protocols
  • To archive a hard copy and electronic copy of all completed medical trainee or medical consultant-initiated academic researches
  • To supervise the technical review and/or approval of all trainee or medical personnel–initiated medical researches before referring to the Ethics Review Committee (ERC) for ethical review as deemed necessary based on the latest versions of the Guideline for Good Clinical Practice and the Philippine National Ethical Guidelines for Health Research
  • To archive a hard copy of the final Technical and a hard copy of the Ethical approval certifications of all original medical trainee or medical consultant-initiated academic protocols
