Cardinal Santos Medical Center

Philippine Center for Advance Surgery

The Philippine Center for Advanced Surgery houses the state-of-the art facilities for training on Minimally Invasive Surgery (MIS) which will include wet and dry skills laboratories, training rooms and an auditorium which can seat 150 people.

MIS is a surgical procedure in which operations are performed using specially designed surgical instruments and endoscopes through very small incisions compared to the traditional open surgery of before. This procedure is becoming a more popular treatment option amongst surgeons because of its beneficial effects on their patients – faster recovery time, less post-operative pain and scarring and shorter hospital stay.

Atty. Pilar Nenuca P. Almira, CSMC President and CEO, envisions the hospital to be the country’s premier academic training institution and leader in Minimally Invasive Surgery.

Cardinal Santos Medical Center has been active in MIS conventions and skills training workshops here and abroad. For the past 3 years, CSMC has partnered with PALES (Philippine Association of Laparoscopic and Endoscopic Surgeons) through its annual conventions bringing live feed of MIS procedures in the hospital to the convention site.

Presently, CSMC has two surgical theaters dedicated solely for minimally invasive procedures. In 2012 alone, there were 118 cases of laparoscopic and endoscopic procedures performed by distinguished CSMC surgeons.

With the launch of PCAS, more surgeons are expected to be trained on proper techniques and instrumentation for minimally invasive surgical procedures allowing more Filipino patients to have better access to the latest surgical treatment innovations while ensuring patient safety and quality of healthcare.
