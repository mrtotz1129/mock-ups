CSMC First to Acquire InMotion Robotics Solutions in PH

Cardinal Santos Medical Center marked another milestone as the first hospital to acquire InMotion Interactive Robotics System that uses advanced robotic technology to help patients recover from neurologic injuries.

The CSMC Rehabilitation Medicine Center is the first to acquire the robot-assisted therapy that provides rehabilitation technology for the hand, wrist, and shoulder therapy of patients with stroke, multiple sclerosis, and other neurological conditions. This technology was developed through research in medical engineering at the Newman Laboratory for Biomechanics and Human Rehabilitation at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).

The InMotion Robotics uses the same movements that a patient performs with a therapist’s guidance. However, instead of the traditional therapy approach, it uses video games that guide patients through clinician-selected exercises. With this state-of-the-art approach, the patients can significantly increase their exercise repetitions resulting to improved performance and faster recovery. By applying repetitive and intensive exercises, the InMotion Robotics helps patients regain normal movements and function.

The InMotion Robotics System is designed for easy use and preparation for patients. It also has an interactive feature that adapts with the patient’s changing ability and capacity. The robots are compact and can function in many different hospital settings.

The InMotion Robots in the CSMC Rehabilitation Medicine Center available are:

  • Shoulder-Elbow Robot that helps improve elbow and shoulder control.
  • Wrist Robot which allows three degrees of movement. It can be adjusted for the use of paediatric patients as well.
  • Hand Robot which helps patients with “grasp, release, and pinch” exercises.

With the InMotion Robotics System, patients now have a bigger chance of getting back to their normal movements and way of everyday living.
